Alabat, Quezon

A 5th class municipality in the Province of Quezon, Philippines in the southern part of Luzon (Region-IV-A CALABARZON). It is a town located in the center of the Island - Perez in the northwest and Quezon in the southwest; Pacific Ocean in the east and Lamon Bay in the west.


It boasts of warm water, scenic beauty, flora and fauna, clean environment, beaches, as well as mountain ranges for hiking and camping and of course, the warm hospitality of Alabatin.


The name Alabat came from “ALABAT” (a tagalog word) which means balustrade. The early inhabitants of this place used to make balustrade across their doors to prevent babies from falling down from ladders of their nipa hut (bahay kubo). Alabat is a paradise island and the Pacific because of its excellent fishing grounds, productive soil, wide plains and nearness to rivers and safe landing shore.

How to Reach Alabat

It takes five (5) hours from Manila which is 257 km

Bus (JAC or AB Liner) going to Atimonan, Quezon or any bus going to Bicol.

In Atimonan, take a RoRo (Roll-on, Roll-off) vessel or any motorized vessel. It would take almost an hour (7.4 nautical miles) from Atimonan Port to reach Alabat Port.
